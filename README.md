# Executive Decision Simulator – Online Retail Startup

## Overview
This project presents an Executive Decision Simulator designed for a small online retail startup selling eco-friendly products. The simulator models one year of business operations and evaluates strategic management decisions using simulated data, scenario analysis, and interactive dashboards.

The objective is to demonstrate how data analytics can support executive decision-making in small and medium enterprises.

---

## Business Context
Small online retailers often face trade-offs between marketing investment, staffing, operational efficiency, and customer satisfaction. This project simulates realistic monthly business metrics and evaluates how different strategic decisions influence performance outcomes such as profit and customer experience.

---

## Dataset
A synthetic dataset representing 12 months of operations was generated with the following variables:

- Revenue (£)  
- Cost (£)  
- Profit (£)  
- Staff Count  
- Marketing Spend (£)  
- Customer Satisfaction (%)  
- Inventory Level  
- Website Traffic  

The dataset mimics realistic SME operational patterns and financial relationships.

---

## Strategic Scenarios

### 1. Marketing Boost
- Marketing spend increased by 20%  
- Expected revenue increase of 10%  
- Objective: growth through demand generation  

### 2. Staff Expansion
- Two additional employees hired  
- Customer satisfaction increased by 5%  
- Objective: improve service quality and customer experience  

### 3. Cost Optimization
- Operational costs reduced by 10%  
- Objective: improve profitability through efficiency  

---

## Analytical Approach
The simulator evaluates each scenario using:

- Financial performance (profit trends)  
- Customer experience metrics  
- Comparative scenario dashboards  
- Executive scoring model combining profit and satisfaction  

**Weighted decision score:**  
Score = 0.6 × Profit + 0.4 × Customer Satisfaction  

This reflects typical executive priorities balancing financial and customer outcomes.

---

## Key Insights
- Marketing investment increased revenue but required monitoring of cost impact.  
- Staff expansion improved customer satisfaction but reduced short-term profit.  
- Cost optimization produced the strongest profit improvement with minimal satisfaction impact.  

Overall, efficiency improvements delivered the most balanced performance outcome in this simulation.

## Interactive Dashboard

These dashboards enable executives to explore “what-if” strategic decisions.

## Recommendation
Based on combined financial and customer metrics, the simulator identifies the most effective strategy for the modeled SME environment.

The approach demonstrates how decision simulation can support data-driven management in small businesses.
